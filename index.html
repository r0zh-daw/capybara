Anatomy

They have a long, stout body with short legs and a round head. Their eyes and nostrils are positioned on top of their head, which allows them to see and breathe while mostly submerged in water. They have thick, coarse fur that can range in color from reddish-brown to yellowish-brown.


Capybaras have large, sharp incisors that they use for gnawing on vegetation, and they have webbed feet that help them swim. They are social animals and can be found in groups in close proximity to water sources.


Overall, the anatomy of a capybara is well-adapted for their semi-aquatic lifestyle and for foraging on land for vegetation.
